## Analysis Settings

For the current analysis, we utilized the **Pass8 (P8R3)** algorithms and data spanning 17 years, from August 4, 2008, to August 2,  2025. A binned maximum likelihood analysis was conducted with a bin size of 0.1°, employing the latest standard Fermi Science Tools \*\*(2.4.0) \*\*software package. We selected events with energies ranging from \*\*100 MeV to 1 TeV \*\*within a region of interest (ROI) of 10° around the position of each VHE cluster, utilizing the P8R3_SOURCE_V6 instrument response functions (IRF).

## Binned Likelihood Lightcurves

This section shows the generated likelihood-binned light curves for all ten VHE clusters. The light curves span a period of 17 years. Three different binnings were tested ( 17, 34, and 68 bins ) corresponding to 1 year, 6 months and 3 months, respectively. Additionally, the Bayesian Blocks method was applied to the generated light curves to quantify the variability of each cluster and to characterize their activity states. These were overplotted as red blocks on the light curves.

<Note>
  The table below shows per‑bin results from a likelihood-based yearly binned light-curve analysis for Cluster ID-97 . For each time bin it lists the bin midpoint in Mission Elapsed Time (MET) and the bin duration (Delta_Time), followed by the measured photon flux in units of ph cm⁻² s⁻¹ (Flux) and its 1sigma uncertainty (Delta_Flux).
</Note>
